FAQ

  • I feel anxious about speaking in public, what can I do?

    • This is something that happens to every person sooner or later. My approach helps, first of all, to obtain emotional and psychological relief, which prevents you from speaking. Further, this is all consolidated by practice. The practice of not only speaking itself, but also the feeling of lightness and comfort while doing it.

  • What if it's too late to start?

    • People study not only at school or university, people study throughout their lives: for pleasure or out of necessity. People of all ages, from 6 to 75 years old, maintain their language level. Remember, it's never too late! I am waiting for you, I will be glad to see you!

  • How do I overcome the fear of speaking?

    • Start with simple conversations with language exchange partners, for example, with me. Join language meetups or online communities to practice. Remember that making mistakes is a natural part of the learning process.

  • How long will it take to become fluent?

    • It varies for each individual. Factors include the language's difficulty, your native language, and the amount of time you dedicate to learning. Focus on gradual improvement rather than setting strict timelines.
